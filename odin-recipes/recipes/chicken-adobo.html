Famous Chicken Adobo

Description

Chicken Adobo is a beloved and iconic Filipino dish known for its rich, tangy, and savory flavors. It typically consists of chicken pieces (often bone-in and skin-on) marinated and simmered in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns. The key to this dish is the marinating process, which allows the chicken to absorb the bold flavors of the marinade before it's slowly cooked to perfection. The resulting dish has a wonderful balance of salty and tangy notes with a hint of sweetness, making it both comforting and incredibly appetizing.

Chicken Adobo is not just a culinary masterpiece but also a cultural symbol in the Philippines, where it's considered a national dish. It's enjoyed in various regional variations, with some recipes incorporating coconut milk or potatoes for added richness and texture. Served over steamed rice, Chicken Adobo embodies the essence of Filipino cuisine, showcasing the country's diverse culinary influences and creating a truly delightful and satisfying dining experience.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 (3 pound) chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 large onion, quartered and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons minced garlic
  • ⅔ cup low sodium soy sauce
  • ⅓ cup white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 2 teaspoons black pepper
  • 1 bay leaf

Steps

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook chicken pieces until golden brown, 2 to 3 minutes per side. Transfer chicken to a plate and set aside.
  2. Add onion and garlic to the skillet; cook until softened and brown, about 6 minutes.
  3. Pour in soy sauce and vinegar and season with garlic powder, black pepper, and bay leaf.
  4. Return chicken to pan, increase heat to high, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until chicken is tender and cooked through, 35 to 40 minutes.
